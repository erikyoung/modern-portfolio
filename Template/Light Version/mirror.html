Mobile ECommerce

Target User

The primary users of Mirror are people who are looking for clothes in a convenient, online experience that provides them with maximum information about the product. The users are primarily working adults who may or may not have children. Most users will be conscious of price, so offerring sales is attractive to them

Goal

The primary goals was to develop a logical shopping experience that provides maximum information for the user with minimal effort. It also places special emphasis on creating a checkout process that breaks down each step and makes it clear to the user about what is happening before they make a final decision.

My Role

UX/UI Designer & Researcher

Summary

Mirror is a very big clothing store chain for both kids and adults. They also have different styles--casual, business, sporty, party, and formal. Mirror is looking for a website that does more than simply give it a web presence. For years they have lived on the back of a static, non-responsive website, but now they are looking to take the leap to something more engaging, more thoughtful, and of course, more user-friendly. In short, they are looking for a way to improve the user experience of their site and to drive sales in the digital space by improving time and money spent with each user session.


Through the process of developing this site, it was discovered that users want an easy to use resource for shopping. Many of them found shopping online to be difficult and shied away from it, mostly because it couldn’t match the real-time experience in terms of understanding the feedback. That became the main mission of the site: maximum info for the user.



Process

Research

Research began by looking at the current landscape of online booking for clothes with a number of different brands, both clothing as well as other industries. The goal was to see how people are overcoming this virtual/physical environment barrier that tends to plague the online shopping experience. Considerable time was spent looking at case studies as well as information about consumer habits before beginning any kind of primary research.

When we began the primary research phase, we inverviewed 6 people, ranging in age from 21 to 51 to get a diverse range of perspectives from people who buy products or regularly do any shopping. During the interviews, the emphases of our interviews was on their recollection of a recent shopping experience. Through this retelling of their experiences, we looked deeply at the motivations behind certain purchases and understanding the drivers that went into certain purchases as well as what pain points came up during their shopping experience. We then used this information to inform the main content of our page and how we intended to proceed with building out the site.

As a general summary, we discovered that purchases are not done randomly; rather, they are part of a variety of circumstances, recent decisions and experiences, and even marketing that has led to their purchase. On top of that, we also discoverd what they want out of a shopping experience as well: maximum info and maximum convenience.

Interaction & UI Design

.


Getting to the wireframes was a fairly straightforward process because we knew what the users wanted: lots of sales, lots of suggestions, lots of choices. We knew these were priorities, so our landing page needed to express that. Aside from that, the bulk of our page from then on primarily reflected the user flow.


From a UI perspective, what we realized for this site was that much of the content was going to be occupying the user’s eye, so lots of colors and emphasis on others aesthetics was not going to be a primary concern for us; rather, let the products do the talking for us.

Reflection

This site was more straightforward than others given the established nature of ecommerce shopping sites. There is a lot of information that exists about this kind of site and has been done countless times by people. The real trick was finding a slick way to communicate maximum information to the user without the experience being too complicated for the user. In the end, however, I I think we came out with something solid that conveys a good shopping experience that meets most needs.
